Mortgage Calculator — Canada & US

Our mortgage calculator is built specifically for Canadian and American homebuyers. For Canada, it uses the legally required semi-annual compounding formula under the Interest Act, calculates CMHC mortgage insurance premiums for down payments under 20%, and shows your stress test qualifying payment. Canadian Mortgage Basics

In Canada, mortgage interest is compounded semi-annually by law (twice per year), not monthly like in the US. This means the effective monthly rate is lower than simply dividing the annual rate by 12. The correct formula is:

Monthly rate = (1 + annual rate / 2)^(1/6) − 1

If your down payment is less than 20% of the purchase price, you are required to purchase CMHC mortgage default insurance. The premium ranges from 2.80% to 4.00% of the loan amount depending on your down payment percentage, and is added to your mortgage principal.

Canadian lenders also apply a mortgage stress test: you must qualify at the higher of your contract rate plus 2%, or the minimum qualifying rate of 5.25%. Our calculator shows both your actual payment and your stress test payment.

US Mortgage Basics

In the United States, mortgage interest is compounded monthly. The monthly rate is simply the annual rate divided by 12. Standard US mortgages are typically 15 or 30 years. Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) is required if your down payment is less than 20%.
